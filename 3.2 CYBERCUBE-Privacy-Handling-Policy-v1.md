0. Purpose & Design Principles

This policy establishes how CYBERCUBE handles personal data across all
operations. It ensures compliance with privacy regulations, protects
individual rights, and maintains trust with customers and users.

Regulatory alignment:

- GDPR (EU General Data Protection Regulation)
- CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act/Rights Act)
- UK GDPR (post-Brexit)
- LGPD (Brazil)
- Other applicable privacy laws

Design principles:

1. **Privacy by Design** â€” Build privacy into all systems
2. **Data Minimization** â€” Collect only what's necessary
3. **Purpose Limitation** â€” Use data only as stated
4. **Transparency** â€” Be clear about data practices
5. **Accountability** â€” Document and demonstrate compliance
6. **Individual Rights** â€” Respect and facilitate rights

document are to be interpreted as described in RFC 2119.

3.3 Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA)

Required before relying on legitimate interest:

```markdown
## Legitimate Interest Assessment

### Processing Activity
[Description of processing]

### Purpose Test
What is the legitimate interest?
- [ ] Business interest identified
- [ ] Interest is lawful
- [ ] Interest is clearly articulated

### Necessity Test
Is processing necessary for the purpose?
- [ ] Processing achieves the purpose
- [ ] No less intrusive way exists
- [ ] Processing is proportionate

### Balancing Test
Do individual rights override the interest?
- [ ] Nature of data considered
- [ ] Expectations of individuals assessed
- [ ] Impact on individuals evaluated
- [ ] Safeguards implemented

### Outcome
- [ ] APPROVED â€” Legitimate interest valid
- [ ] REJECTED â€” Use different basis or don't process
```

9.3 High-Risk Factors

Indicators of high risk requiring individual notification:

- Special category data exposed
- Financial data exposed
- Authentication credentials exposed
- Large number of individuals affected
- Data could enable identity theft
- Data could enable fraud
- Vulnerable individuals affected

9.4 Notification Content

**Authority notification must include:**

- Nature of breach
- Categories and approximate number of individuals
- Categories and approximate number of records
- DPO contact details
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken/proposed

**Individual notification must include:**

- Nature of breach in clear language
- DPO contact details
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken/proposed
- Recommendations for individuals

## Appendix A: Glossary

This glossary defines key terms used throughout the CYBERCUBE Privacy Handling Policy. All definitions are normative unless stated otherwise.

**Adequacy Decision** â€” EU Commission determination that a third country provides adequate data protection. Countries: UK, Switzerland, Canada, Japan, South Korea, etc.

**Anonymization** â€” Irreversible process removing all identifiable information. Result: Data no longer considered personal data.

**Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)** â€” Internal rules for data transfers within multinational organizations. Alternative to SCCs for intra-group transfers.

**CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)** â€” California privacy law granting consumer rights over personal information. Amended by CPRA (California Privacy Rights Act).

**Consent** â€” Freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous agreement. GDPR Art. 4(11): Must be affirmative action.

**Controller** â€” Entity determining purposes and means of processing. CYBERCUBE role: Controller (own data), Processor (customer data).

**Cookie** â€” Small text file stored on user's device. Types: Strictly necessary, functional, analytics, advertising.

**Data Breach** â€” Security incident affecting personal data confidentiality, integrity, or availability. Notification: GDPR 72 hours, CCPA per Cal. Civ. Code Â§1798.82.

**Data Minimization** â€” Collecting only data necessary for specified purpose. GDPR Art. 5(1)(c).

**Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** â€” Risk assessment for high-risk processing activities. Required for systematic profiling, large-scale special categories.

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)** â€” Designated privacy compliance expert. Required when: large-scale monitoring or special categories.

**Data Subject** â€” Identified or identifiable natural person. Synonym: Individual, consumer, user.

**DSAR (Data Subject Access Request)** â€” Request to exercise data subject rights. Response time: GDPR 30 days, CCPA 45 days.

**Erasure (Right to)** â€” Right to have personal data deleted. Synonym: Right to be forgotten (GDPR Art. 17).

**Explicit Consent** â€” Consent given through clear affirmative statement. Required for special categories, cross-border transfers.

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)** â€” EU regulation on data protection and privacy. Effective: May 25, 2018.

**High-Risk Processing** â€” Processing likely to result in high risk to rights and freedoms. Triggers DPIA requirement.

**Lawful Basis** â€” Legal ground for processing personal data. GDPR Art. 6: Consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests.

**Legitimate Interest** â€” Lawful basis where processing serves controller's legitimate purposes. Requires Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA).

**Personal Data** â€” Any information relating to identified or identifiable natural person. Examples: Name, email, IP address, location, device ID.

**Personal Information (PI)** â€” CCPA term equivalent to personal data. Includes: Identifiers, commercial info, internet activity, geolocation.

**Privacy by Design** â€” Building privacy into systems from the outset. GDPR Art. 25.

**Privacy Notice** â€” Document informing individuals about data processing. Synonym: Privacy policy (external).

**Processing** â€” Any operation performed on personal data. Includes: Collection, storage, use, disclosure, deletion.

**Processor** â€” Entity processing data on behalf of controller. CYBERCUBE role: Processor for customer data.

**Profiling** â€” Automated processing to evaluate personal aspects. Examples: Performance, reliability, behavior, location.

**Pseudonymization** â€” Processing so data cannot be attributed without additional information. Reversible (unlike anonymization).

**Purpose Limitation** â€” Data collected for specified, explicit, legitimate purposes. GDPR Art. 5(1)(b).

**Rectification (Right to)** â€” Right to correct inaccurate personal data. GDPR Art. 16.

**Retention Period** â€” Duration personal data is kept. Principle: No longer than necessary.

**Sale (of Personal Information)** â€” CCPA/CPRA: Disclosure for monetary or valuable consideration. Consumer right: Opt-out of sale or sharing.

**Sensitive Personal Information** â€” Data requiring heightened protection. CCPA: SSN, financial, health, biometric, precise geolocation.

**Special Category Data** â€” GDPR Art. 9 data requiring explicit consent or exemption. Includes: Racial origin, health, biometric, political, religious.

**Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** â€” EU-approved contract terms for international transfers. Version: 2021 SCCs (modular).

**Sub-Processor** â€” Processor engaged by another processor. Requires controller authorization.

**Third Party** â€” Entity other than data subject, controller, or processor. Examples: Partners, vendors, service providers.

**Transfer** â€” Disclosure of personal data to recipient in another country. Requires adequate safeguards.

**Transparency** â€” Clear information about data processing provided to individuals. GDPR Art. 12-14.
